AI-PDLC Weekly Workflow

Product uses AI agents to prepare artifacts. On Monday, product and engineering collaborate to turn them into a plan. Devs build all week. Friday, the whole team demos, reviews, and polishes together. Faster discovery. Faster requirements. Faster builds.

Build & Test Cycle
The engineering loop

Adapted from McKinsey's Accelerated AI Product Development Approach. This is the continuous cycle engineering runs within each week.

Track 1 — Product

Async, AI-Assisted Discovery

Product owners use a pipeline of AI agents covering user research, market requirements, journey maps, PRDs, and prototypes. Some weeks it's the full flow. Most weeks it's targeted — updating a journey map, refining a PRD, generating mocks for a specific feature. By Friday, the week's specific artifacts are ready for the EM.

Track 2 — Engineering

Continuous Engineering Execution

On Monday, devs work alongside product to deeply understand the users, the problem, and the artifacts. EM has already organized the work into stories on the board. Devs build Tue–Thu, demo Friday morning, and polish Friday afternoon.

Weekly Cadence
The week at a glance

Monday and Friday are collaborative ceremonies where product and engineering work together. The middle of the week is heads-down build time. Product runs agents async throughout.

Monday
1 hr meeting
Refinement / Sprint Planning
Morning — EM Prep
  • EM reviews artifact package received Friday — feature-specific PRD, Figma frames, prototype spec, journey maps
  • EM pulls Figma frames directly into LLM via Figma MCP, reviewing design intent alongside the PRD
  • EM creates epics & stories in Jira MCP directly from the Figma frames and PRD — each story references the specific frame and acceptance criteria
  • Stories scoped for LLM-compatible development so devs can pull the same Figma context while building
Meeting — Full Team
  • PO walks the team through user context, problem space, and key artifacts
  • Team reviews the board — questions, adjustments, dependencies, priorities
  • Work is organized and understood — devs pull from the board as capacity allows
  • Devs communicate what will be completed and demoed by Friday
  • Teams can choose to size stories, use agents to size stories, or just use stories completed for metrics
viability build & test em prep 1 hr meeting ceremony collaborative
Tue — Thu
full days
Build + Product Prep
  • Devs pull stories from the board and use LLM plan mode to plan story completion in LLM
  • LLM executes the plan — writing code, building features, and refactoring as needed
  • Devs review the generated code for correctness, architecture, and edge cases
  • LLM testing skill generates tests to team standards — devs review and verify coverage
  • Update Jira status as work moves through the pipeline
  • Async by default — sync only when blocked
  • EM monitors board, unblocks, stays out of the way
Meanwhile — Product
  • Runs McKinsey AI-PDLC agents to prepare next week's artifacts
  • Conducts or synthesizes user interviews
  • Drafts and reviews MRDs, PRDs, mocks, and prototype specs
  • Iterates until the artifact package is ready to deliver to EM on Friday
build & test discovery viability heads down jira = source of truth product preps next week

1 hr
Demo
  • Live software demos against acceptance criteria & mocks
  • PO gives real-time feedback
  • What shipped, what needs tweaks, what carries over
  • Resolve Trivial Feedback
  • Take non-trivial PO feedback and create stories to resolve
build & test ceremony live demos only

2–3 hrs
Tweak & Polish

  • PO works alongside devs for rapid clarification

End of Day — Next Week Prep
  • PO delivers next week's artifact package to the EM — PRD, mocks, prototype spec, journey maps
  • EM has the weekend + Monday morning to review, refine, and organize the board

Roles
Who does what
◈
Product Owner
Operates the 9-agent pipeline. Reviews every agent output before it feeds into the next. Delivers the full artifact package to the EM end of day Friday. Walks the team through user context and problem space in Monday's planning meeting. Provides real-time feedback at Friday demo. Partners with devs on Friday afternoon polish. Owns prioritization and the hypothesis tracker.
◆
Engineering Manager
Receives artifacts from product end of day Friday. Reviews Figma frames via Figma MCP in LLM alongside the feature-specific PRD. Creates epics & stories in Jira MCP directly from the frames and PRD. Facilitates the 1-hour Monday refinement meeting. Monitors the board and unblocks during the week. Runs Friday demo. Tracks throughput and flow. Protects build days from interruption.
▣
Developers
Review the board in Monday's planning meeting — ask questions, flag concerns, clarify priorities. Pull stories and build in LLM as capacity allows. Update Jira status. Write tests as part of story completion. Demo their work Friday. Partner with product on Friday afternoon polish.

Measurement
Measurement (Based on DX Core 4 Framework)

Four metrics measured across the weekly cadence. The first three are observable within the workflow itself. Business impact requires a longer time horizon and lives with product.

Speed
Throughput
Stories completed per week on the kanban board. Visible in Jira. The simplest measure of whether the pipeline is flowing.
Measured
Weekly via kanban tool
Effectiveness
Non-Trivial Demo Issues
Count of non-trivial issues surfaced in Friday demo. High count signals a breakdown in communication between product artifacts and engineering understanding. This measures the quality of the collaboration, not just the output.
Measured
Every Friday demo
Quality
Client-Found Defects + Test Coverage
Count of client-found defects (CFDs) that make it past the team into production. Test coverage as the leading indicator, CFD count as the lagging one. Together they measure whether the human-verifies-AI loop is catching things.
Measured
Count of CFDs, post-release
Business Impact
Product-Defined Outcomes
Success criteria defined by product in the MRD and hypothesis tracker (revenue per dev, ROI, innovation ratio). The agents generate the success criteria, the product team verifies whether they were met.
Measured
Owned by product, longer horizon

Every artifact is AI-generated. Every artifact is human-verified.
The agents do the work. The team — product and engineering — own the outcome together.
